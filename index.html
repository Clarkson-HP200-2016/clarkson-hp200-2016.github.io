Observation Tower

The self-standing observation tower was designed with ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) accessibility in mind, and is fully wheelchair accessible. The tower will be located on the south side of the south side of the Zenda Farms property, and can provide breathtaking views of the surrounding areas such as the nearby wetlands, field of grass nesting birds, and the Saint Lawrence River. The top deck of the tower will be equipped with interpretive signage that informs visitors on the historical significance and ecological impact of these surrounding areas. The foundations of an old farm building are also located near where the tower will be located, which will help tell the history of Zenda Farms.

Apple Orchard

The apple orchard at Zenda farms will feature a unique, family-oriented atmosphere including educationally enhanced activities everyone will enjoy. The location of the orchard is still TBD but will include a 5 acre plot dedicated to growing cider apples and a 2 acre plot dedicated to eating apples. The cider apple varieties will include Golden Russet and Freedom apples while the eating apple varieties will include Honeycrisp, Marlin Stephens, and Liberty apples. Activities planned for the orchard will include the following: apple picking, an autumn themed apple festival, orchard tours and wagon rides, as well as cider pressing demonstrations on a small scale. Visitors will be able to walk around the orchard and pick their own apples in various quantities or purchase already picked apples and apple products in the farm store. We will offer wagon rides and tours around the historical property that will be accessible for everyone at a low cost to customers. Zenda also plans to host an annual autumn apple festival where families can come to the property and participate in fall activities such as pumpkin decorating, apple picking, corn maze, candy apple making, other small games. These attractions are a great opportunity for the community to get involved with a local organization and have a good time!

Hiking Trail

Zenda’s South parcel trail system showcases the restored wetlands and wildlife on Zenda’s beautiful South parcel of land. The one and a half mile trail encircles the wetlands while providing views of the St. Lawrence River, Zenda’s historic silver Jamesway buildings, and the native bird life in the wetlands.

The quarter mile handicap accessible trail leads visitors from the trailhead to a bird blind, which provides views of these wetland birds without startling them. A one and a quarter mile trail from this point leads visitors around the wetlands over the rolling hills and meadows. On the east portion of this trail is a trail branch that leads to another bird blind along the wetland. Zenda’s South parcel trail system has something for everyone and is sure to provide a fulfilling experience to all visitors.

Nature Playground

The children’s play area provides an environment which allows children to safely explore and experience nature while also encouraging children to learn about the native plant and animal species which inhabit Jefferson County. The children’s play area features a playground constructed of natural materials which highlight the natural beauty which is present throughout Zenda Farms. This area includes an outdoor classroom which can utilized for demonstrations, field trips, and other large group activities. This area also provides interactive programming ranging from arts and crafts projects to a scavenger hunt which includes various stations throughout Zenda Farms.The children’s area also features a pollinator garden comprised of colorful, flowering plants which allows both children and adults to observe various species of butterflies, bees, and birds in their native habitats.

Creamery Renovation

The Creamery is one of the buildings with the most potential on the Zenda Farms Preserve. It will become the welcome center for Zenda, offering visitors information, maps, farm produce and products, and a location to participate in programming activities. Each room will be renovated in order to best fit the business plan, including adding a kitchen, an office, a entry room/store, and a multipurpose, classroom-style, space. The renovation of the creamery will be transformative to the Zenda Farms Preserve experience, as it will provide a central location to be the face of the preserve.

CSA

Zenda’s CSA (Community-Supported Agriculture) Garden is in its preliminary stages as a small-scale vegetable source for families in the Clayton area. Engagement in the CSA program encourages our community members to become more aware and involved in the agricultural process on the local level. Members are allotted weekly shares of the garden’s harvest--available pick-up right on site in Zenda’s historical creamery. Educational tours are also available for interested individuals or groups. We encourage anyone interested in participating in or learning more about our CSA program to reach out to us! We would be more than happy to help you help us grow!

Meet Our Awesome Team

Jake Tibbles
Executive Director

Jake was appointed Executive Director in May 2012. He earned a bachelor’s degree in Environmental Science from the State University of New York at Cortland, majoring in both Biology and Chemistry. He first came to the Land Trust in 2007 in a research internship and continued on as Director of Stewardship. Since being appointed Executive Director, he has overseen TILT's Reaccreditation by the Land Trust Alliance Accreditation Commission, and its growth in conserved lands, in educational programs, and staffing.

A native of Redwood, NY, Jake has a life-long passion for the environment and especially the Thousand Island region. He hikes, fishes, hunts and enjoys water sports. He has a keen appreciation for the importance of the St. Lawrence River system and is pleased to have the opportunity to work in its behalf.

Rebecca Dahl
Preserve Coordinator

Rebecca oversees the activities at Zenda Farms Preserve, including the development of a business plan for the future of the Preserve as a sustainable farm and environmental and agricultural learning center. She comes to TILT with experience at the farm facilities at UCONN, as well as zoos in CT and NJ.

Most recently, she worked at Fossil Rim Wildlife Center in Glen Rose, TX, developing interpretive programming, and focusing on sustainable agriculture. She grew up in New Jersey and is a graduate of the University of Connecticut.
